# David Sinclair: Can Aging Be Reversed?

## TL;DR

David Sinclair, Harvard biologist who has studied aging for 30 years, argues that **aging is not wear-and-tear but information loss**: epigenetic noise gradually causes cells to forget which type they are. His lab has shown they can manufacture aging on demand in young mice (the [[ICE Mouse Experiment]]) and reverse it in old mice using three of the [[Yamanaka Factors]]. The first human age-reversal clinical trial launches this year, treating a form of blindness. He predicts a pill within years that resets biological age. Practical lifestyle takeaway: trigger your body's "[[Adversity Mode]]" through fasting, intense exercise, temperature extremes, and stressed plants.

## Core arguments

### I. Aging is information loss, not damage

The conventional view treats aging as wear-and-tear — parts breaking down. Sinclair argues this is wrong: the hardware (DNA) is fine. **The software (the [[Epigenome]]) is corrupted.**

> Every cell in your body has the same DNA. What makes them different is which genes are turned on. Aging is the gradual loss of *that information*. The cells slowly forget which genes to read.
> —— [[David Sinclair]]

The mechanism: every chromosome break (which happens constantly) pulls [[Sirtuins]] off their normal job of keeping cell-identity genes silent, sending them to the repair site. Most return to their post. Some don't. **Over decades, this accumulated "drift" is what we experience as aging.**

### II. The ICE mouse: manufacturing aging on demand

Sinclair's lab built a mouse engineered to produce small, controlled chromosome breaks on a switch. The breaks healed perfectly — no DNA damage. **The only change was that sirtuins kept getting pulled off their jobs.**

The result: young mice rapidly aged — grey fur, organ deterioration, cognitive decline, old-looking hearts. **Aging without damage.** This is the proof of the [[Information Theory of Aging]].

> "That's not a sick mouse. That's an old mouse."
> —— [[David Sinclair]] (recalling the moment his lab confirmed the theory)

### III. Reversal: from mice to the first human trial

Activating three of the four [[Yamanaka Factors]] in adult cells doesn't turn them into stem cells — it makes them *remember* their original cell type. In old mice, this restored:
- Vision
- Cognitive function
- Organ function
- Female fertility (mice past reproductive age started ovulating again)

**Lifespan extension in already-old mice: ~100%**, with full functional quality (running, mating, behaving young).

> This year — 2024 — we're starting the first human clinical trial. We're treating people with a disease that causes blindness in middle age. We'll inject these factors into the eye. If it works, they will see again.
> —— [[David Sinclair]]

The reset works **repeatedly** on the same animal — no apparent ceiling. The current method requires gene therapy, but small molecules ("a pill") are being developed.

### IV. Why didn't evolution fix aging?

Aging persists not because it's optimized but because **evolution has no incentive to keep you alive past reproduction**. Aging is an absence of selection pressure, not a deeply tuned process. **This is why intervention is possible** — there's nothing biologically sacred about decay.

### V. The Adversity Mode framework

All effective lifestyle interventions share one mechanism: **convince your body it's in danger**, which triggers longevity programs (sirtuin activation, autophagy, NAD elevation).

- **Intermittent fasting** (16/8 or skip a meal) → "I'm starving"
- **HIIT exercise** (out-of-breath intensity) → "I'm exhausted"
- **Cold exposure** (plunges, cold showers) → "I'm freezing"
- **Sauna** (4-7×/week — Finnish data shows dramatic mortality reduction) → "I'm overheating"
- **Stressed plants** ([[Xenohormesis]] — wild/organic, bitter, colorful) → eating their stress molecules
- **Pulse-dosed supplements** → keep the body off-balance, prevent adaptation

### VI. Sinclair's personal stack

| Compound | Dose | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) | 1g morning | Boost [[NAD]] for sirtuin function |
| Resveratrol | 1g with yogurt (for absorption) | Sirtuin activator |
| Metformin | 1g evening | Blood sugar regulation |
| Vitamin D3 + K2 | Standard | General health |
| Statin | Low dose | Cardiovascular |
| Aspirin | Low dose | Inflammation |

He tracks biological age regularly via blood-based epigenetic tests.

## Counterintuitive points

- **Aging is not damage.** It's information loss. The DNA is fine; the cells just forget which genes to read.
- **Cancer is also an identity crisis.** A cancer cell is, fundamentally, a cell that has forgotten what it was supposed to be — same root cause as aging.
- **Female fertility can be reversed**, at least in mice. Old mice past reproductive age started ovulating again after epigenetic reset.
- **Stressed plants are healthier for you than perfectly-grown ones.** The stress molecules they produce ([[Polyphenols]]) become your medicine ([[Xenohormesis]]).
- **The body's healing systems only activate when it thinks it's in danger.** Comfort is the enemy of longevity.

## Action items

1. **Start with intermittent fasting** — skip a meal a day, eat in an 8-hour window. Highest-leverage, free, immediate.
2. **Exercise to the point of being out of breath** — walking is good for general health but doesn't trigger longevity programs. HIIT does.
3. **Use a sauna 4+ times a week** if you have access. The Finnish mortality data is overwhelming.
4. **Eat the rainbow, prefer wild/organic.** Don't fear bitter foods — bitterness signals the defensive compounds you want.
5. **Pulse-dose, don't daily-dose.** Vary supplements and even some lifestyle factors. Adaptation kills the benefit.
6. **Track biological age** if you can — services exist for this. Without measurement, you can't know if any of this is working.
7. **Don't wait for the pill.** The lifestyle interventions today can give you 10-20 healthy years. The clinical trials will take years to mature.
